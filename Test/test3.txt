4: Following the SWOT model to this project. I analyzed: 

Strengths

Accessibility: Mobile apps offer greater flexibility, allowing students to register for courses anytime, anywhere.

User Experience: Modern UI/UX design can enhance student satisfaction and engagement.

Speed: Mobile apps generally load faster than web-based systems, improving the overall efficiency.

Innovation: Positions the university as a forward-thinking institution, boosting its reputation.

Weaknesses

Development Costs: Developing and maintaining a mobile app can be more expensive.

Tech Limitations: Not all students may have access to smartphones, which could limit usage.

Learning Curve: Users accustomed to the old system may find the transition challenging.

Security Risks: Mobile platforms can be more vulnerable to certain types of cyber attacks.

Opportunities

New Features: Add functionalities like push notifications for deadlines, real-time updates, and easy access to support.

Market Expansion: Can attract more students, especially those who prioritize tech-savvy institutions.

Feedback Integration: Easier to collect and implement user feedback for continuous improvement.

Partnerships: Collaborate with tech companies for better features and sponsorships.

Threats

Technical Issues: Bugs and crashes could deter users and tarnish the app's reputation.

Competitor Advantage: Other universities might develop superior apps, reducing competitive edge.

Compliance: Must ensure the app complies with privacy laws and university policies.

Adoption Resistance: Users resistant to change could stick with the old system or cause friction during the transition.
